Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

In 1968, Intel was established by Noyce and Moore, revolutionizing how we think about integrated circuits and semiconductors. Their early innovations set a course for the tech industry’s future.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

The 4004 packed 2,300 transistors onto a single chip, running at 740 kHz. It was originally designed for a calculator but became the spark for the modern computing era.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

The 8086 introduced 16-bit processing and became the foundation for the entire x86 family for decades.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking.

The 386 enabled advanced multitasking and set the stage for the x86 standard that dominates PCs today.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations.

Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals.

The RISE strategy aligns innovation with sustainability and inclusivity across global operations and the supply chain.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2040.

Building on years of environmental initiatives, Intel pledges carbon neutrality.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

Partnerships and long-term procurement enabled this milestone.

The 2024 Summit tackled breakthrough technologies, policy alignment, and sustainable manufacturing best practices.
